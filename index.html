Why Haiku?

Haiku has existed as a Japanese art form since the 7th century, and has many different forms in Japanese; however, in Western culture these poems are typically expressed in a 5-7-5 syllable count, as you'll find them here.

English is a complex language in the sense that you can have things spelled the same way, but sound completely different. Take, for instance, the words cough, through, tough, and bough. They're spelled the same, but they're all pronounced differently.

Haiku is simpler in the sense that it doesn't rhyme; however, it can be difficult to count syllables, since haiku is always written in a 5-7-5 on; an on is essentially a phonetic sound in the Japanese language. While Japanese words can carry more meaning, it provides a unique challenge to create whole concepts in Western haiku.
